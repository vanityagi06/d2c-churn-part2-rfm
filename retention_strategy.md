# Retention Strategy
## Objective
The objective of this segmentation exercise is to identify customer groups with different behavioral patterns and recommend targeted retention strategies that maximize retention impact while operating within a limited campaign budget.

## Champions
Characteristics:
* High frequency purchases
* High spending
* Recent activity

Retention Action:
* VIP rewards
* Early access to product launches
* Exclusive member-only offers

Priority:
Medium

## Loyal Customers
Characteristics:
* Frequent purchases
* Consistent engagement
* Strong brand affinity

Retention Action:
* Loyalty points
* Referral incentives
* Personalized recommendations

Priority:
Medium

## Potential Loyalists
Characteristics:
* Recent activity
* Moderate purchasing behavior
* High future value potential

Retention Action:
* Cross-sell recommendations
* Personalized product suggestions
* Limited-time engagement campaigns

Priority:
High

## At Risk Customers
Characteristics:
* Long recency
* Declining engagement
* Reduced purchase activity

Retention Action:
* Re-engagement campaigns
* Retention discounts
* Customer feedback surveys

Priority:
Very High

## Dormant Customers
Characteristics:
* Extremely inactive customers
* No recent engagement

Retention Action:
* Win-back campaigns
* Special comeback offers
* Personalized email outreach

Priority:
High

# Budget Recommendation
Assuming a retention budget of ₹100,000:
| Segment             | Budget Allocation |
| ------------------- | ----------------- |
| At Risk             | 40%               |
| Dormant             | 30%               |
| Potential Loyalists | 20%               |
| Loyal Customers     | 5%                |
| Champions           | 5%                |

## Justification
At Risk and Dormant customers have the highest probability of churn and therefore provide the greatest opportunity for retention impact. Retaining these customers is more cost-effective than acquiring new customers.
